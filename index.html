ABOUT

PERSONAL PROFILE

Lilian Imoni is a multifaceted and multi-talented personality who has built a career in the Brands and Project Management workspace spanning almost a decade of corporate work experience. Her motivation to create and build brands inspired her career path and has played a huge role in everything she does, she is inspired by an inner conviction of a “bigger picture”, is goal-oriented, and believes that there is a natural course to life that is irrespective of human feelings – “you give, you toil and reap and what is yours will come to you”. This philosophy enables her to run a mentor-staff relations program in her organization, where everyone is taught to focus on passing through and harnessing talents that will open them up to bigger opportunities. 

Having spent most of her formative years in Ibadan, Oyo State, with Primary education starting in 1986 at Command Children’s School, Mokola, Ibadan. She attended St Louis Girls’ Grammar School, Ibadan, where she completed her secondary education in 1999, and moved on to get her first degree in business administration from one of Nigeria’s finest universities. It was while at the university as a student that she started her professional career in PR/ marketing and Advertising working with Lordsgate communications in 2002 as a marketing executive and event coordinator; she was part of projects like the Benson & Hedges Roadshow tour (Abuja) and British Council “Connecting Futures” program in Kaduna all as an undergraduate. She also co-founded a non-Profit called ASPIRE - African Students for Impact and Relevance.

Lilian is one of Nigeria’s fine minds when it concerns ideas and strategy in today’s business, a trained and certified project manager from London Business Academy, she is currently studying a Mini MBA in Business Skills Competency from the London school of business and finance. She has a diploma in International business and economics from the Institute of Business, Berlin.

A brands analyst with a penchant for all things that require in-depth analysis and communication from thought to finish. She was nominated in 2017 by Africa’s Premiere Brand Strategist, Charles Otudor as a Brand to Watch alongside other reputable Brands like Uche Nnaji, Ali Baba, Steve Harris, Latasha Lagos, Ronke Rockafella, and Richard Mofe Damijo (RMD).

She has worked with various top brands in industries ranging from Finance to Advertising and Entertainment. Some notable brands are Pearl Awards where she served for five years as the Head of sponsorship and marketing, Omaness Skinfood as the Brand Evangelist, AWLO as Head of Brands and Project Management, CITC as Monitoring and Evaluation Manager, and Head of Corporate Communications. She managed the Charles Otudor Brand Master Class event in 2017 and 2018 with over 1000 participants and delegates in attendance from across Nigeria. She also manages some personal Brands like Kenneth Okolie, Mr. Nigeria 2010 and Leading Nollywood actor, Casey Edema (winner of Glo Naija sings 2010), Funmi Eko Ezeh, a Nollywood thespian.

Currently, she serves as the Chairman of the Local Organizing Committee for the inaugural Pan African Brand Awards (PABA), an award event boasting of participation of over 15 African countries. She is the chief errand officer of Lillyville Management Company, an integrated brands and project management firm. She's served as the Head, Corporate Communications of women shaping Africa (W.O.S.A) and Visioner of Women in sustainable enterprise (W.I.S.E) Initiative geared towards impacting our society through real-time support and collaboration. She was featured as a woman of Rubies in the Guardian woman edition 2016.

She has a flair for TV production and has featured in some series and produced some as well, she also hosts the famous family talk show Amazons on the radio with classic FM Lagos. She is the presenter of The Amazons on TV till date.

She debuted her platform as a columnist in Timeless Magazine “High heels with Lillian”, dedicated to motivating young women in business, through the inspiring stories of trials, temptations, and success stories of thriving business women across the land, she is inspired by her mistakes.
